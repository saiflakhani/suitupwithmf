My Experience

CONSTANTINE CANNON LLP, LONDON

I primarily worked with the Art and Cultural Property law team but also assisted the Competition and Whistle Blower teams with tasks as needed. My main tasks included legal research, drafting chronologies, client emails, document requests, research notes, engagement letters, blog post updates and internal strategy documents. I assisted the members in business development tasks by researching and creating knowledge update presentations, first drafts and proof-reading articles, brainstorming ideas, and strategies for improvement. I had the opportunity to develop with two other members a social media strategy for the Art and Cultural Property law team. During my internship I presented on two topics – 5th Anti Money Laundering Directive in the UK and what the new AML law in the USA can learn from its implementation; and Non-Fungible Tokens (NFTs) and the art market.

SHARDUL AMARCHAND MANGALDAS, MUMBAI

I worked with Dr. Rishab Gupta and his team in a virtual setting. My main tasks were researching and proofreading. I also assisted the team in an ICSID Arbitration filing, a new experience for me. In my last week I was given the opportunity to write a detailed article on the new LCIA Rules 2020, outlining the key changes and pros and cons of the same.

GENERAL CORPORATE, TRILEGAL, MUMBAI

Litigation, contract and insurance due diligence for a proposed share purchase transaction. I also performed research to identify the rules applicable to a company that falls under 2 categories of the new FDI Policy. I also assisted a senior associate with preparing general insurance guidelines checklist for a proposed due diligence. Since the internship was cut short due to the covid-19 lockdown, Trilegal designed a 4-part webinar series on the basics of M&A in which I participated.

KHAITAN & CO, MUMBAI

I worked on various types of disputes involving both litigation and arbitration matters. My main tasks included drafting ancillary documents for filing, proofreading, research on specific points of law ranging from interpretation of grounds for challenging an arbitration award to recovery of an already adjudicated debt against a company in liquidation. I frequently visited High Court and NCLT with seniors for matters and for senior counsel briefings to understand the court procedure, make notes of proceedings and share circulate summaries. At the end of my internship, I presented on “The Psychology of Arbitration – Understanding how to deal with Arbitrator bias”.

SHARDUL AMARCHAND MANGALDAS

I worked with Dr. Rishab Gupta and his team on various international disputes involving construction arbitration, indemnity claims, foreign investment in India, section 34 petition, enforcement of foreign awards in India, contract law, labour disputes, etc., attended arbitration and court proceedings. I also wrote the first draft of a book chapter on Indian Arbitration that Dr. Gupta was collaborating on and a presentation on the Singapore Mediation Convention that was signed in August, 2019.
